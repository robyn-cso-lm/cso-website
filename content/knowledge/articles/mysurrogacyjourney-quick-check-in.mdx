Had my monthly OB appointment yesterday and was a little nervous to talk about weight gain. In all seriousness I am still 2.5lbs under my “pre-pregnancy” weight, but I was down 14lbs so I am back up 11.5lbs. I just need to remind myself that at this stage with my own kids I was already up 20+ lbs so I am WAY ahead of the game. Doctor said nothing about my weight and seemed quite happy… the nurse actually told me to eat more often throughout the day Ha!

I took a “weekly bump photo” today and cannot believe how big this thing is for being 22 weeks! I am measuring 2.5 weeks ahead of what I am (which is also new to me) but that could have a few different reasons…. a large amount of amniotic fluid is one and I completely agree with that. When I had Rosie the nurses had made a comment that they have never seen so much fluid accompany a birth, made her going head down difficult and it didn’t actually happen until labour started but all worked out

Sent a message to the parents today. I had a few things on my mind that I felt I needed to know the answer to before my head exploded.

1) How are they planning on feeding this baby? Usually a baby has its “first feed” 30-60 minutes after birth. Will they be giving her a bottle of formula? Are they wanting me to provide milk for then or perhaps the first couple weeks?

2) What are their expectations of the labour and birth itself? Are they planning on being with me through it all? Through just the pushing? Maybe they don’t want to be there at all and wait until she is actually born? Maybe only the mother wants to be part of the whole process? I will admit, if I ever do this again those are some questions I will be asking before any pregnancy happens.

My IPs are amazing and I really have to expectations nor am I all for, or all against anything so I really need their guidance here. I love birth and the whole process so it really doesn’t matter to me who is there to experience it…. just be ready to feel my wrath during transition

I am a little afraid of the first question… My body is terrible at producing (well keeping) milk, so I would hate to be set up for failure. I am pretty confident I could supply a weeks worth but after that is anyones guess. I also am not sold on the whole pumping every 2 hours thing…. especially in the middle of the night. But like I said, a week or so I could do

Phil made the comment the other day of “so, when you are in labour I just stay at home with the kids? Do the parents see you when you’re in labour or just after the baby is born?” ok Phil, I get its a little complicated and uncharted territory for all of us but do you honestly think I am labouring and pushing out a baby ON MY OWN?! I’m pretty sure he thought he was getting out of this one haha. Boy was he wrong

Baby girls movements are much stronger now. She is a relaxed baby, she never gets too crazy in there…. i think she is going to be a wonderful infant

I was actually able to see a large kick last night while watching tv. Now we are getting into the fun part of pregnancy! I can’t wait for the rest <3
