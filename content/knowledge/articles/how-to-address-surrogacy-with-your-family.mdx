If you are interesting in becoming a surrogate, you likely going to face some very serious conversation and perhaps even some that are intimating. Surrogacy is such a big decision that it’s important to voice your opinion on your choice, and work with the significant people in your life to decide if surrogacy will be a good fit in your life.

Here are some tips for bringing up the idea of surrogacy to your family and how to handle it:

Do as much research as possible, and be able to show it

You need show what surrogacy is, how it will affect your current situation, the type of surrogacy you want to participate in and what the effects will be on yourself. Explain why you have chosen to do this.

Make sure you listen, and be prepared to hear different points of view

After you have explained your side of the situation, it’s your turn to listen. Listen respectful and do your best to understand their point of view. They might not have the same opinion as you, and they also might need to time to warm up to the idea. So allow them to get out their own feelings. If it doesn’t go well the first time, give it a few days to sit.

Try to keep emotions to a minimum

Do your best to not allow your emotions to get the best of the situation. Allowing emotions to overtake the conversation will keep you from getting out everything you want to say and it will change the direction of the conversation as well.

Resist the urge to argue

They may have conflicting opinions about your choice, and you will need to accept this. Don’t allow the conversation be a reason to get upset with one another. Arguments could decrease your chance for showing a person with conflicting views why surrogacy can be something amazing.

It’s important to not be afraid to address a conversation like this. Go into it confidently and without any expectations. You never know how the other person will react. If you would like more information or guidance on how to address surrogacy with your family, feel free to reach out to us!
