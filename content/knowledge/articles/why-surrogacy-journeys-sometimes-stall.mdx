# Why Surrogacy Journeys Sometimes Stall

*Five common pressure points, and how better preparation can help*

Surrogacy journeys are emotional, complex, and deeply personal.

When a journey slows down, a match ends, or a transfer does not move forward as expected, it can feel as though everything has suddenly gone wrong. In reality, there is rarely one single reason.

Some complications cannot be predicted or prevented. Medical outcomes are never guaranteed, and a failed embryo transfer is not anyone's fault.

But many delays, disrupted matches, and difficult misunderstandings can be reduced when the right questions are asked early.

Here are five common pressure points we see in surrogacy journeys.

## 1. Medical readiness is confirmed too late

A potential surrogate may look like a wonderful match personally, but the fertility clinic must still determine whether she meets its medical requirements.

Previous pregnancy records, delivery history, current health, medications, and clinic-specific criteria can all affect whether a surrogate is medically approved.

When medical records are incomplete or reviewed late, intended parents and surrogates may become emotionally invested before eligibility is confirmed.

Early record collection and clinic review can help everyone move forward with clearer expectations.

## 2. Expectations are not discussed before matching

A strong match involves more than liking one another.

Intended parents and surrogates should discuss communication, travel, appointments, pregnancy decisions, birth expectations, privacy, termination and selective reduction, contact after delivery, and the kind of relationship they each hope to have.

There does not need to be perfect agreement on every small detail, but the major expectations must align.

It is much easier to have an honest conversation before matching than to discover a significant difference later.

## 3. Financial planning is incomplete

Surrogacy includes more than an agency fee.

Families may also need to prepare for clinic costs, legal fees, insurance, travel, psychological assessments, surrogate reimbursements, escrow, and unexpected expenses.

Financial uncertainty can create enormous stress during an already emotional process.

A realistic budget, clear payment milestones, and access to funds before each stage help protect both intended parents and surrogates.

## 4. Communication becomes inconsistent

Silence can quickly create fear.

An intended parent may wonder whether a surrogate has changed her mind. A surrogate may feel forgotten. A family may assume nothing is happening when medical records, legal documents, or clinic approvals are still moving behind the scenes.

Regular communication does not mean there will always be new information. Sometimes the update is simply that the process is still underway.

Clear expectations about who will communicate, how often, and through which method can prevent uncertainty from becoming mistrust.

## 5. Legal and logistical details are left too late

Every surrogacy journey involves legal and practical planning.

Contracts, independent legal advice, insurance, escrow, clinic coordination, travel, birth planning, and parentage steps all need to be addressed at the appropriate time.

International and cross-border families may also need to consider citizenship, immigration, passports, embryo transport, and where the baby will be born.

These details are manageable, but they should not be treated as last-minute paperwork.

## Preparation cannot guarantee an outcome

No agency can promise pregnancy, a successful transfer, a specific matching date, or a complication-free journey.

What good preparation can do is reduce avoidable confusion, identify concerns earlier, and help intended parents and surrogates make informed decisions before becoming deeply committed.
