Egg donation is one of the most powerful tools in reproductive medicine — and in Canada, it occupies a unique legal and ethical space. Whether you're a family who needs an egg donor or a person considering donating, understanding the Canadian framework is essential.

Let's start from the beginning.

## How Egg Donation Works in Canada

In a donor egg cycle, eggs are retrieved from a donor, fertilized with sperm (from a partner, co-parent, or donor), and the resulting embryo is transferred to the uterus of the intended parent or a gestational surrogate.

Egg donation is commonly used by:

- Women who cannot produce viable eggs due to age, premature ovarian failure, or genetic conditions
- Same-sex male couples and single fathers pursuing surrogacy
- Women with a history of repeated IVF failure

## The Legal Framework

Canada's *Assisted Human Reproduction Act* (AHRA) governs egg donation at the federal level. Key things to understand:

**Altruistic donation only.** Canadian law prohibits the purchase or sale of human eggs. Donors cannot be paid for their eggs, but they can be reimbursed for reasonable expenses incurred during the donation process — including travel, medical appointments, medications, and lost wages.

**Donor screening is rigorous.** All egg donors in Canada undergo extensive medical and psychological screening before donation proceeds.

**Anonymous vs. open-identity donation.** Canada has moved toward open-identity donation practices. Children born through donation have the right to access donor identity information when they reach adulthood, which is worth understanding before you proceed.

## Finding an Egg Donor in Canada

This is often the most challenging part for intended parents. There are several pathways:

- **Agency-facilitated matching** — an agency can help you connect with donors who have already completed preliminary screening
- **Known donors** — some families use a friend or family member as a donor; this requires additional legal and psychological considerations
- **Fertility clinic databases** — some clinics maintain their own donor pools

Because of the altruistic-only framework, the pool of available donors in Canada is smaller than in countries where compensation is permitted. Wait times for a matched donor can range from a few months to over a year.

## Costs to Expect

While you cannot pay for eggs, there are still significant costs involved:

- Donor medical screening
- Donor reimbursement for expenses (varies by donor and circumstances)
- Medications for both donor and recipient
- Egg retrieval procedure
- IVF cycle costs
- Legal agreements

Total costs for a donor egg cycle can range from $25,000 to $45,000 or more, depending on the clinic and circumstances.

## A Word to Potential Donors

If you're considering donating your eggs, first: thank you. The families who receive egg donations often describe it as the most transformative gift they've ever received.

Donation involves a medical process — hormonal stimulation and egg retrieval under sedation — that carries some medical risk. Before you proceed, make sure you have thorough consultations with a reproductive specialist and a counsellor experienced in third-party reproduction.
