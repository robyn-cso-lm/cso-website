## Why There's a Shortage

Surrogacy in Canada is growing — more families are seeking it, more clinics are equipped to support it, and awareness of the process has expanded significantly in recent years.

But surrogate recruitment has not kept pace.

The reasons are understandable. Most women don't grow up thinking "someday I'll be a surrogate." It's not a path that's actively advertised or normalized. Many women who would be *extraordinary* surrogates have never seriously considered it — not because they wouldn't do it, but because they've never been asked.

## Who We're Looking For

You don't have to have it all figured out. You don't have to be certain. You just have to be curious and willing to learn more.

The basic eligibility criteria for surrogacy in Canada:

- You are between 21 and 45 years old
- You have given birth to at least one child of your own
- You are in good physical and mental health
- You are a Canadian citizen or permanent resident
- You do not smoke (or are willing to quit)
- You have a support system — a partner, family, or close friends who are behind you

That's the starting point. Everything else — the medical screening, the legal process, the matching — happens with full support from our team.
