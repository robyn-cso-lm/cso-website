Common Questions

Answered honestly.

What does Camica's agency fee cover?

The agency fee covers surrogate sourcing, screening, matching, legal coordination on both sides, medical cycle coordination, full case management from match through birth, and post-birth support. Surrogate compensation, medical costs, and legal fees are separate and paid directly — Camica coordinates but does not mark them up.

How is Camica different from a large agency?

Large agencies process dozens of families at a time. Camica is deliberately small. Robyn personally reviews every match and is reachable by phone. You are not a file number. The founding period specifically limits intake so that every family gets the attention they came for.

What does the Founding Families deposit lock in?

The $2,500 deposit locks in today's package rate for the life of your journey — rates will increase as Camica grows and demand rises. It also puts you at the front of the matching queue ahead of families who join after the founding period closes. The deposit is credited in full toward your package fee at match.

Can I use my own fertility clinic?

Yes. Camica coordinates with your existing reproductive endocrinologist. If you are in Canada, your embryos stay at your Canadian clinic and are transported to Florida for the transfer under standard protocols. You do not need to change clinics.

What if we do not match within 12 months?

Your $2,500 founding deposit is refunded in full. We have a deep Florida surrogate pool and have not had difficulty matching families who are reasonably flexible — but the guarantee is there regardless.

Is the Hybrid Pathway (Canadian birth) available for US families?

The Hybrid Pathway is designed specifically for Canadian intended parents who want their child born in Canada. US families typically deliver in Florida with their surrogate. Camica offers full US-based surrogacy programs — your package includes clinic coordination and full legal support in your state.
