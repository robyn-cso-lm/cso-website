Questions We Hear Every Time

Answered honestly.
No glossing over.

Is a Florida surrogate legally valid in Canada?

Yes. The legal framework for cross-border surrogacy is well-established. A Canadian reproductive attorney handles the Ontario parentage order. Your child is born in Canada and recognized as yours under Canadian law. Camica has reproductive attorneys on both sides coordinating the process.

Why is my baby born in Ontario and not Florida?

We designed the Hybrid Pathway specifically so that Canadian intended parents do not have to travel. Your surrogate travels to Ontario at 36 weeks for delivery. You are home. Your baby is born in Canada, holds Canadian citizenship, and the Ontario birth order is obtained here.

What if the surrogate doesn't want to travel to Canada?

This is part of the matching criteria and is fully contracted before any transfer occurs. Camica only matches you with surrogates who have agreed to the Ontario delivery arrangement. It is not a surprise — it is part of the original agreement they sign.

How is this different from a US agency?

Comparable US boutique agencies charge $38,000–$65,000+ for an agency fee alone, without the Canadian-birth structure. Camica's Founding Families fee is $24,500 USD, includes cross-border coordination, and credits your existing CSO investment. It is built for Canadian families, not American ones.

What happens to my embryos — are they safe to move?

Your embryos stay where they are. The surrogate's transfer is done at a Florida fertility clinic. Embryo transport protocols are well established. Camica coordinates with your clinic to ensure proper handling. Your reproductive endocrinologist remains your medical team throughout.

What if we don't match in 12 months?

Your $2,500 founding deposit is fully refunded. No conditions. We have never had an issue matching families who are reasonably flexible — but we believe you should have that protection regardless.
