Requirements

Are you a good fit?

We are selective for good reason. The families we work with are trusting a surrogate with the most important thing in their lives. Here is what we look for.

  • Between 21 and 40 years old
  • Have given birth to at least one child you are currently raising
  • Non-smoker (no tobacco or vaping for at least 12 months)
  • BMI under 32 at time of screening
  • No history of serious mental health conditions
  • No felony convictions
  • Financially stable (not receiving government assistance)
  • Located in Florida, or willing to carry in Florida
  • Support system in place (partner, family, friends)

Common concerns.

Here is what most women ask us before they apply. Honest answers, nothing glossed over.

"Will the baby feel like mine?"

Gestational surrogacy means the embryo comes entirely from the intended parents. There is zero genetic connection to you. Most surrogates describe the experience as deeply meaningful without the emotional confusion.

"What about my own kids while I'm pregnant?"

Childcare allowances are built into your contract. You live your normal life. The pregnancy is planned around your family, not the other way around.

"What if I change my mind partway through?"

That is exactly what legal contracts and our thorough screening process are designed to prevent. We invest heavily in making sure you are fully prepared before a single embryo is transferred.

"Is this safe?"

Gestational surrogacy is as safe as any planned pregnancy. You have proven you carry healthy pregnancies. Full health and life insurance is in place before transfer.
